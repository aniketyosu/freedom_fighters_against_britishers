Freedom Fighters of Chhattisgarh

Honoring the brave souls who fought for India's independence.

Veer Narayan Singh (1795–1857)

Veer Narayan Singh, a landlord from Sonakhan, Chhattisgarh, was the first martyr from the region in the 1857 revolt against British rule. He led a rebellion with 500 men and was executed at Jaistambh Chowk in Raipur. His sacrifice made him a tribal leader and a symbol of resistance in Chhattisgarh.

Thakur Pyarelal Singh (1891–1954)

Thakur Pyarelal Singh was a prominent freedom fighter and labor leader from Rajnandgaon, Chhattisgarh. He led several workers' movements against British exploitation and was instrumental in promoting education and cooperative societies in the region. His dedication earned him the title "Tyagmurti" (epitome of sacrifice).

Pandit Sundarlal Sharma (1881–1940)

Pandit Sundarlal Sharma was a key figure in Chhattisgarh's independence movement. He initiated the Canal Satyagraha in 1920, raising political and social consciousness among the masses. His efforts laid the foundation for future movements in the region.

Ghanshyam Singh Gupta

Ghanshyam Singh Gupta played a significant role in laying the foundation of the independence movement in Chhattisgarh. His contributions were pivotal in mobilizing the local population against British colonial rule.
