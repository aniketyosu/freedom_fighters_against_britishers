Freedom Fighters of Bihar

Honoring the brave souls who fought for India's independence.

Veer Kunwar Singh (1777–1858)

Veer Kunwar Singh was a prominent leader during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. At the age of 80, he led armed forces against the British East India Company and became a symbol of resistance in Bihar. His bravery and leadership inspired many in the fight for freedom.

Dr. Rajendra Prasad (1884–1963)

Dr. Rajendra Prasad was a key figure in India's independence movement and the first President of independent India. Born in Bihar, he was deeply involved in the non-cooperation movement and worked closely with Mahatma Gandhi. His dedication to the nation is remembered with great respect.

Saraswati Devi (1901–?)

Saraswati Devi, known as the Iron Lady of Bihar, was a fearless freedom fighter who participated actively in the Quit India Movement. Her contributions to the struggle for independence are a testament to the vital role women played in India's freedom journey.

Abdul Bari (1892–1947)

Professor Abdul Bari was a prominent freedom fighter from Bihar who played a significant role in promoting communal harmony and labor rights. He was instrumental in organizing workers and advocating for their rights during the British colonial period.

Maghfoor Ahmad Ajazi (1900–1966)

Maghfoor Ahmad Ajazi was a political activist from Bihar who was deeply involved in the Indian independence movement. He was known for founding the All-India Jamhur Muslim League and was a strong advocate for the Urdu language.

Syed Mohammad Sharfuddin Quadri (1901–2015)

Syed Mohammad Sharfuddin Quadri was an Indian independence activist and a physician of the Unani system of medicine. He participated in the Salt March of 1930 alongside Mahatma Gandhi and was imprisoned for his involvement in the freedom struggle.

Karpoori Thakur (1924–1988)

Karpoori Thakur was a freedom fighter and a prominent political leader from Bihar. He actively participated in the Quit India Movement and later served as the Chief Minister of Bihar, advocating for social justice and upliftment of the marginalized.
