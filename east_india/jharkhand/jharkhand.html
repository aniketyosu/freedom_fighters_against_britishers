Freedom Fighters of Jharkhand

Honoring the brave souls who fought for India's independence.

Birsa Munda (1875–1900)

Birsa Munda, born in Ulihatu, was a tribal leader who led the Ulgulan movement against British rule. He aimed to establish Munda Raj and fought against the exploitation of tribal communities. His efforts led to the Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, protecting tribal land rights. Birsa Munda remains a revered figure in Jharkhand's history.

Sidhu Murmu (c.1815–1856) & Kanhu Murmu (c.1820–1856)

Sidhu and Kanhu Murmu were brothers who led the Santhal Rebellion in 1855–56 against British colonial authority and the oppressive zamindari system. Mobilizing around 10,000 Santhals, they challenged British forces in present-day Jharkhand. Though the rebellion was suppressed, it remains a significant chapter in India's freedom struggle.

Tilka Manjhi (c.1750–1785)

Tilka Manjhi was a tribal leader from the Santhal community who initiated an armed rebellion against British rule in the 18th century. He attacked British officials and resisted their policies, becoming one of the earliest freedom fighters in India. Tilka Manjhi was eventually captured and executed, but his legacy endures.

Pandey Ganpat Rai (1809–1858)

Pandey Ganpat Rai was a zamindar from Lohardaga who played a crucial role in the 1857 revolt against British rule. He organized local forces and collaborated with other leaders to challenge colonial authority. His leadership and sacrifice are remembered as a testament to Jharkhand's contribution to India's independence.

Nana Tana Bhagat (Dates Unknown)

Nana Tana Bhagat was a freedom fighter from the Ranchi district who led the Tana Bhagat Movement. This movement combined religious reform with political resistance, advocating for non-violence and the rights of tribal communities. His efforts contributed to the broader struggle against British colonialism in Jharkhand.
