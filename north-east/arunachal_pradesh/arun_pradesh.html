Freedom Fighters of Arunachal Pradesh

Matmur Jamoh

Matmur Jamoh was a legendary warrior from the Adi tribe of Arunachal Pradesh. He led a bold resistance against British colonial officers in 1911. Along with other tribal warriors, he ambushed and killed British officer Noel Williamson and his companion Dr. Gregorson near Komsing village. Moje Riba

Moje Riba was a courageous patriot from the Adi community and an early political activist who supported the Indian National Congress. He is renowned for hoisting the Indian national flag in Dambuk in 1947, symbolizing the spirit of freedom and self-rule before India officially attained independence. His act inspired many and made him a prominent figure in Arunachal’s freedom narrative.

Talom Rukbo

While not a direct combatant, Talom Rukbo played an essential role in the socio-cultural resistance against colonial and external influences. A reformer and spiritual leader, he worked to preserve indigenous faith, unity, and identity among the tribes of Arunachal Pradesh. His work laid a strong foundation for cultural confidence and awareness that paralleled the nationalistic movement.

Tebo Ege

Tebo Ege is remembered in local oral traditions as a tribal leader who resisted British incursions into indigenous territories. Though historical documentation is limited, his leadership in protecting tribal autonomy from colonial interference marks him as a hero among his people.
