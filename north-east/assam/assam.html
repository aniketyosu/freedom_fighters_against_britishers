Freedom Fighters of Assam

Honoring the brave souls who fought for India's independence.

Maniram Dewan (1806–1858)

Maniram Dewan was a prominent Assamese nobleman and the first Indian tea planter. Deeply involved in the 1857 uprising, he was arrested and hanged by the British for his role in the rebellion. His martyrdom is a significant chapter in Assam's history. [Source]

Kanaklata Barua (1924–1942)

Kanaklata Barua, affectionately known as Birbala, was a young freedom fighter who became an iconic figure in the Quit India Movement of 1942. Born in Barangabari, Assam, she was deeply influenced by the nationalistic fervor of the time. [Source]
