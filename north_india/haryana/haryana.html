Freedom Fighters of Haryana

Honoring the heroes who fought valiantly against British colonial rule.

Rao Tula Ram (1825–1863)

Rao Tula Ram was a prominent leader during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. As the ruler of Rewari, he played a crucial role in resisting British forces in Haryana. After facing defeat, he sought international support against the British but passed away in Kabul in 1863. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Nahar Singh (1823–1858)

Nahar Singh, known as the Lion of Ballabhgarh, was a valiant ruler who joined the 1857 uprising against British rule. His leadership and bravery were evident as he fought fiercely but was eventually captured and executed in 1858. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Banarsi Das Gupta (1917–2007)

Banarsi Das Gupta was a dedicated freedom fighter who faced imprisonment multiple times during the British era. He founded the Praja Mandal in Jind State, advocating for responsible governance. Post-independence, he served as the Chief Minister of Haryana, continuing his commitment to public service. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Pandit Neki Ram Sharma (1887–1956)

Pandit Neki Ram Sharma was deeply involved in India's freedom struggle, participating in movements like the Non-Cooperation and Quit India Movements. His relentless activism led to multiple imprisonments, totaling over 2,200 days. He later served as a Member of Parliament, representing Haryana. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Deshbandhu Gupta (1901–1951)

Deshbandhu Gupta was a journalist and politician who actively opposed British rule. He was instrumental in advocating for press freedom and played a significant role in the Constituent Assembly of India. His efforts were pivotal in shaping post-independence India's democratic framework. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Babu Mool Chand Jain (1915–1997)

Known as the 'Gandhi of Haryana', Babu Mool Chand Jain was a staunch Gandhian who participated in the Individual Civil Disobedience Movement. He endured severe injuries during protests and later served in various political roles, including Finance Minister of Haryana. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Sir Chhotu Ram (1881–1945)

Sir Chhotu Ram was a prominent leader who championed the rights of farmers and the underprivileged. He established the Zamindar League and was instrumental in forming the Marketing Board in 1938, facilitating better grain sales for farmers. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Rai Bahadur Lala Murlidhar (1850–?)

Lala Murlidhar was an early participant in the Indian National Congress and actively opposed British policies. In protest against the Rowlatt Act, he renounced his British-conferred titles, showcasing his commitment to India's freedom. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Pandit Shri Ram Sharma (1899–?)

Pandit Shri Ram Sharma was a dedicated freedom fighter who took part in all major Satyagraha movements between 1921 and 1942. He also contributed to the freedom struggle through his writings, publishing the 'Haryana Tilak' weekly and authoring works on Haryana's history. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Sucheta Kripalani (1908–1974)

Born in Ambala, Sucheta Kripalani was a prominent figure in the Quit India Movement. She later became the first woman to serve as the Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh, breaking significant political barriers for women in India. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
