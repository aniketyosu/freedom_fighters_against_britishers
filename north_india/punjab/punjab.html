Freedom Fighters of Punjab

Honoring the heroes who fought for India's independence from British rule.

Bhagat Singh (1907–1931)

Bhagat Singh was a revolutionary icon who galvanized India's youth against colonial oppression. His fearless acts, including the assembly bombing and his execution at 23, made him a martyr and symbol of resistance. His legacy continues to inspire generations.

Lala Lajpat Rai (1865–1928)

Known as the 'Lion of Punjab,' Lala Lajpat Rai was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement. He led the protest against the Simon Commission and succumbed to injuries from police brutality, becoming a symbol of sacrifice.

Udham Singh (1899–1940)

Udham Singh avenged the Jallianwala Bagh massacre by assassinating Michael O'Dwyer in London. His act was a powerful statement against colonial atrocities, and he was executed in 1940, remembered as a hero of justice.

Sukhdev Thapar (1907–1931)

Sukhdev Thapar was a close associate of Bhagat Singh and played a crucial role in revolutionary activities against British rule. He was executed alongside Bhagat Singh and Rajguru, and is remembered for his unwavering commitment to India's freedom.

Kartar Singh Sarabha (1896–1915)

At just 19, Kartar Singh Sarabha became a key figure in the Ghadar Movement, advocating for India's independence. His execution by the British made him one of the youngest martyrs in the freedom struggle.

Sohan Singh Bhakna (1870–1968)

Sohan Singh Bhakna was the founding president of the Ghadar Party, which aimed to overthrow British rule in India. He spent years in prison for his revolutionary activities and continued to inspire the freedom movement until his death.

Dr. Saifuddin Kitchlew (1888–1963)

Dr. Kitchlew was a prominent leader who opposed the Rowlatt Act and played a significant role in the Non-Cooperation Movement. His activism led to his arrest, and he became a symbol of Hindu-Muslim unity in the fight for independence.

Madan Lal Dhingra (1883–1909)

Madan Lal Dhingra was one of the early revolutionaries who took the fight for India's freedom to British soil. He assassinated a British official in London and was executed, becoming a martyr for the cause.
