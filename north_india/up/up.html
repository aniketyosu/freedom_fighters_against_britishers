Freedom Fighters of Uttar Pradesh

Honoring the brave souls who fought for India's independence.

Mangal Pandey (1827–1857)

Mangal Pandey was a key figure in the Indian Rebellion of 1857. Born in Akbarpur, Uttar Pradesh, he served in the British East India Company's army. His attack on British officers sparked the uprising, marking the beginning of India's fight for independence.

Ram Prasad Bismil (1897–1927)

Ram Prasad Bismil was a revolutionary from Shahjahanpur, Uttar Pradesh. He was a prominent member of the Hindustan Republican Association and played a significant role in the Kakori conspiracy against British rule. His patriotic poems inspired many to join the freedom movement.

Chandrashekhar Azad (1906–1931)

Chandrashekhar Azad was a fearless revolutionary born in Bhavra, Madhya Pradesh, but his activities were prominent in Uttar Pradesh. He vowed never to be captured alive and died fighting British forces in Allahabad, becoming a symbol of courage and resistance.

Begum Hazrat Mahal (1820–1879)

Begum Hazrat Mahal was a courageous leader who played a crucial role during the 1857 uprising. After her husband, Nawab Wajid Ali Shah, was exiled, she took charge of Lucknow and resisted British forces, showcasing exemplary leadership and valor.

Rani Lakshmibai (1828–1858)

Rani Lakshmibai, the Queen of Jhansi, was born in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh. She became a symbol of resistance against British rule during the 1857 rebellion. Her bravery and leadership continue to inspire generations in India's struggle for freedom.

Jawaharlal Nehru (1889–1964)

Born in Allahabad, Uttar Pradesh, Jawaharlal Nehru was a central figure in India's independence movement and became the first Prime Minister of independent India. His vision and leadership were instrumental in shaping modern India.

Dr. Ram Manohar Lohia (1910–1967)

Dr. Ram Manohar Lohia was a prominent freedom fighter and socialist political leader from Akbarpur, Uttar Pradesh. He was known for his advocacy of non-violent resistance and played a significant role in the Quit India Movement.

Ganesh Shankar Vidyarthi (1890–1931)

Ganesh Shankar Vidyarthi was a journalist and freedom fighter from Allahabad, Uttar Pradesh. He used his writings to inspire people against British rule and was martyred while trying to quell communal riots, exemplifying his commitment to national unity.
