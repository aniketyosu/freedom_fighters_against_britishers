Freedom Fighters from Andhra Pradesh

Alluri Sitarama Raju (1897–1924)

Led the Rampa Rebellion (1922–24) against British rule in the tribal regions using guerrilla warfare. He became a symbol of resistance before being martyred by British forces.

Uyyalawada Narasimha Reddy (1806–1847)

One of the earliest revolutionaries in South India, he led a revolt in 1846 against British revenue policies. He was executed after a long battle but inspired future uprisings.

Tanguturi Prakasam Pantulu (1872–1957)

Known as "Andhra Kesari", he was a fearless leader in the Indian freedom struggle and became the first Chief Minister of Andhra State.

Duggirala Gopalakrishnayya (1889–1928)

A key figure in the Non-Cooperation Movement, he inspired masses with powerful speeches and promoted nationalist activities in Andhra Pradesh.

Kanneganti Hanumanthu (1870–1922)

A tribal leader who opposed British tax policies and led a rebellion in the Palnadu region. He was martyred while resisting British forces.

Potti Sreeramulu (1901–1952)

A Gandhian activist who participated in various movements including the Salt Satyagraha and Quit India Movement. He later gave his life for the formation of Andhra state.

Kandukuri Veeresalingam (1848–1919)

Noted reformer who fought social evils during British rule. He promoted women’s education and progressive values through writing and activism.

Vavilala Gopalakrishnayya (1906–2003)

A Gandhian freedom fighter involved in the Civil Disobedience and Quit India Movements. He remained active in public life after independence.

Kasinadhuni Nageswara Rao (1867–1938)

Founded the nationalist newspaper Andhra Patrika and supported the Swadeshi movement. He used journalism to spread patriotic ideals among Telugu people.

Padala Ramachandra Reddy

A freedom fighter from Rayalaseema region, he participated in the Quit India Movement and inspired many youth to resist British rule.

Burgula Ramakrishna Rao (1899–1967)

Participated in the freedom struggle and the Hyderabad liberation movement. He became the first elected Chief Minister of Hyderabad State.
