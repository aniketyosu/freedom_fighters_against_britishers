Freedom Fighters of Goa

Honoring the heroes who fought for Goa's liberation from Portuguese rule.

Dr. Tristão de Bragança Cunha (1891–1958)

Known as the 'Father of Goan Nationalism,' Dr. Tristão de Bragança Cunha was instrumental in exposing the oppressive nature of Portuguese colonialism. His publication, 'Denationalisation of Goa,' highlighted the cultural erosion under foreign rule. He founded the Goa National Congress, aligning Goa's liberation movement with India's broader struggle for independence.

Libia Lobo Sardesai (1924–Present)

Born on May 25, 1924, Libia Lobo Sardesai played a pivotal role in Goa's liberation movement. She co-founded the underground radio station 'Voz da Liberdade' (Voice of Freedom) in 1955, broadcasting pro-independence messages despite the risks. Post-liberation, she became Goa's first Director of Tourism and later its first female lawyer.

Vaman Sardesai (1923–1994)

Vaman Balkrishna Naique Prataprao Sardesai was a dedicated freedom fighter and diplomat. Alongside Libia Lobo, he operated the 'Voice of Freedom' radio station from 1955 to 1961, advocating for Goa's independence. Post-liberation, he served as the Indian Ambassador to Angola and was honored with the Padma Shri in 1992.

Mohan Ranade (1929–2019)

Mohan Ranade was a prominent figure in Goa's armed resistance against Portuguese rule. He led several attacks on Portuguese establishments and was imprisoned for 14 years in Lisbon. His unwavering commitment to the cause significantly contributed to Goa's eventual liberation.

Ravindra Kelekar (1925–2010)

An ardent freedom fighter, Ravindra Kelekar participated in the Indian freedom movement and Goa's liberation struggle. A Gandhian activist, he was also a prolific writer and a staunch proponent of the Konkani language, earning accolades such as the Sahitya Akademi Award.

Luis de Menezes Bragança (1878–1938)

A journalist and nationalist, Luis de Menezes Bragança was a vocal critic of Portuguese colonialism. Through his writings and editorials, he inspired Goans to envision a free and progressive society, laying the intellectual groundwork for the liberation movement.

Purushottam Kakodkar (1913–1998)

An active participant in Goa's freedom struggle, Purushottam Kakodkar worked closely with national leaders to integrate Goa into independent India. Post-liberation, he served as a Member of Parliament, contributing to the region's development.

Anthony D'Souza

Anthony D'Souza was a dedicated freedom fighter whose efforts were instrumental in mobilizing support against Portuguese rule. His activism inspired many to join the cause for Goa's liberation.
